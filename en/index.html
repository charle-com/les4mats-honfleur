N° 01

A table on the quay.

A stone's throw from the Vieux Bassin, Les 4 Mâts drops anchor between the half-timbered houses and the trawlers' masts. A port that smells of iodine, a kitchen that lives off it.

We work with the fishermen along the coast, day to day. What comes in at dawn leaves on the plate at night.

No frozen menu. No hiding sauce. Just the produce, the fire, a vinaigrette.

Simpler than it sounds. Harder than it looks.

N° 03

The crew, on board.

A young brigade, gathered around a single idea: what reaches the plate has been caught, cooked and thought out the same day. Co-founded by Paul and Jade, in their twenties. Around them, a tight-knit team who share the same quiet standard.

"We wanted a kitchen
that feels like our
Normandy summers."

In the kitchen, a young and sharp brigade. In the dining room, a front-of-house team that knows the menu by heart and can name every provenance, every fisherman. No one came up through the classic fine-dining circuit, and that's on purpose. No forced reverence, no frozen menu. Just a quiet standard.

The menu moves with the catch. Plates are meant to be shared. And when you walk in, you're welcomed like friends invited over, not customers to be served.
